Windshield Wiper System Design

The design of the windshield wiper system for the Arma GT required conforming to regulations, which allow for the registration of the completed vehicle, while also designing within the nearly finalized car. This posed extreme space constraints, and limited mounting position availability. Despite the challenging constraints, a design was created allowing for both wipers to be driven from a singular motor. This design is contrary to conventional windshield wipers in that the wipers are driven by independent pull or push rods, whereas most conventional designs link each wiper with a single rod. This allowed for the efficient packaging of this windshield wiper system.

Key Features:

  • Designed linkage geometry within existing constraints to provide proper sweep angles of wipers.
    Result: Desired sweep coverage and synchronization of right and left wipers, driven from one motor, yet with individual push/pull rods.
  • Packaged linkage to mate with the existing mounting locations, while clearing keep out zones such as front wheels and cooling system.
    Result: A compact, effective, and lightweight windshield wiper design.
  • Designed to be manufacturable with off the shelf parts, and plasma cut steel plate.
    Result: Allows for cost effective, efficient manufacturing with ideal servicability in the future.
